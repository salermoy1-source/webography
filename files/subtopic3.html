URL How CDs are used in Different Fields
PMC PubMed Central Fintan J McEvoy Eiliv Svalastoga
Abstract This article talks about how in the medical field CDs are used to show patients images and medical information. These CDs contain DICOM so doctors can see the patients images and medical information attached to it. When it comes to medical information it is important that the CDs can not be altered after they are burned. The problem is that they can be altered and when they are it is hard to tell what has changed. This article has examples of when important medical information is altered and can not be checked with the original. Lastly, the article goes over how a DISCOM would work when using a CD and what the patient or doctor would see when using it.



URL Inventions That Changed Music: The Compact Disc
CMN College Media Network N/A
Abstract Currently CDs are not as commonly used compared to in the 80s and 90s, but record companies are still producing them. When CDs first came out they were a game changer for both companies and consumers alike. CDs were easier to use and people could bring them anywhere to listen to music on their Walkman or portable CD player. Companies would sell CDs at big events so that fans could buy them as souvenirs. The invention also helped radio stations because radio stations would help the stations switch songs faster and have their audience listen to more songs. This text is great for learning how CDs changed the way companies sell albums and how it helped people listen to more music.
